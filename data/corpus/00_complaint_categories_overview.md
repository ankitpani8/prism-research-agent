# Prepaid CX — Complaint Category Taxonomy (synthetic KB)

This internal knowledge base defines the complaint categories used by the prepaid
customer-experience team. Content is SYNTHETIC (no real customer PII). Category
structure and benchmark figures are grounded in public regulator disclosures
(see SOURCES.md): billing is consistently the single largest driver, and service
quality, billing and provisioning together dominate telecom complaints.

Categories tracked:
1. Network & Coverage — dropped calls, no signal, poor indoor coverage.
2. Billing & Charges — incorrect charges, unexpected deductions, missing credits.
3. Recharge & Top-up Failures — payment taken but balance not credited.
4. Data Speed & Throttling — slow data, throttling after fair-use caps.
5. Provisioning & Activation — SIM/number activation delays, porting failures.
6. Complaint Handling — slow resolution, repeated contacts, no callback.
7. Contract & Switching — terms not honoured, difficulty switching/porting out.

Benchmark context (external, cited): billing-type issues have been reported as
the leading complaint category at roughly 46% of issues in one national telecom
ombudsman's annual data; regulators elsewhere report that ~90% of telecom
complaints concern service quality, billing, or provisioning. Use these only as
directional benchmarks, not internal ground truth.
